Our Prediction Methodology

Data Collection and Preprocessing

We use the Diabetes dataset of around 1,00,000 entries which contains several medical predictor variables. The dataset undergoes rigorous preprocessing including:

  • Handling missing values
  • Data normalization
  • Feature selection
  • Data balancing

Machine Learning Algorithms

Decision Tree

A tree-like model of decisions that predicts the target value by learning simple decision rules inferred from the data features.

Advantages: Easy to interpret, handles both numerical and categorical data, requires little data preparation.

Naïve Bayes

A probabilistic classifier based on applying Bayes' theorem with strong (naïve) independence assumptions between the features.

Advantages: Fast prediction, works well with high-dimensional data, requires small amount of training data.

Random Forest

An ensemble learning method that operates by constructing multiple decision trees and outputting the class that is the mode of the classes (classification) or mean prediction (regression) of the individual trees.

Advantages: Handles high dimensionality well, works well with both numerical and categorical data, performs well on datasets with missing values.

Model Evaluation

Our models are evaluated using standard metrics to ensure accuracy and reliability:

Accuracy

Measures the proportion of correct predictions among the total number of cases examined.

Precision and Recall

Precision measures the proportion of positive identifications that were correct. Recall measures the proportion of actual positives that were identified correctly.

F1 Score

The harmonic mean of precision and recall, providing a balance between the two metrics.
